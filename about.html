About Me

👋 Hi, I'm Koffivi Fidele Gbagbe — an AI researcher and engineer focused on foundation models for robotics, with a particular interest in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. I created LLMatics as a space to explore, implement, and reflect on the inner workings of generative AI and its applications to real-world robotic systems.

With an academic background in Computer Science and Mathematical Sciences from Skoltech and AIMS Rwanda, and a growing portfolio of publications in multimodal AI, robotics, and LLMs, my research bridges theory and practice in areas like VLA-based robotic control, robot ethical reasoning, and metacognitive frameworks for embodied AI.

On this blog, I break down complex topics like transformer architectures, vision-language grounding, optimization strategies, and reinforcement learning — with hands-on tutorials using PyTorch and TensorFlow. Whether you're a robotics engineer, AI researcher, or a curious learner, LLMatics is a space where theory meets practice.

🔬 I’m currently an AI Research Scientist at CortexTor Labs, where I work on multimodal storytelling, visual reasoning, and causal grounding. I've also contributed to projects in robotic manipulation, drone swarms (ROS2), and AI for healthcare.

💡 LLMatics is where I share tutorials, experiments, code, and insights — aiming to make foundational AI more understandable and actionable.
